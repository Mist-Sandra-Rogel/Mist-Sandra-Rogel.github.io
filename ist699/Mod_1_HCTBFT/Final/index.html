Helpful Communication Techniques Between Familes and ELP Teachers

By Sandra Rogel


Basic Communication Skills

Active Listening

Show genuine interest in what the parent is saying. This involves nodding, maintaining eye contact, and repeating back key points to confirm understanding.


Clear and Simple Language

Employ precise vocabulary and brief sentences to ensure clarity and prevent misunderstandings.


Positive Reinforcement

Acknowledge and commend positive behavior and achievements to foster repetition. Specific praises are more impactful than general ones.


Non-Verbal Cues

Use body language, facial expressions, and gestures to convey warmth and openness.


Consistent Feedback

Maintain consistent communication with parents by providing regular updates on their child's progress and areas needing attention, thereby fostering a collaborative and supportive environment.
